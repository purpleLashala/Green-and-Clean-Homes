Inflation Reduction Act
The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022, was signed President Joe Biden on
August 16, 2022. It is a federal law in the US that is trying to hold
back inflation by maybe decreasing the government's budget shortage,
reducing prescribed drug costs, and supporting household energy
building while furthering clean energy. This act alters and
increases the clean energy investment tax credit, so that up to 30%
credit will be available for approved investments in renewable
energy projects that meet prevailing wage standards and recruit
enough qualified apprentices from registered apprenticeship programs.
This act qualifies for low income families.
The Residential Clean Energy Tax Credit
The Residential Clean Energy Tax Credit, covers about 30% of the cost of
new and clean energy for your home that is inputted from 2022 to 2032.
The credits percentage for property inputted in 2034 it decreases
to 22%. Around 2032, homeowners that will let up to $3200 yearly to
decrease the cost of new energy inputs by up to 30%, will be eligible
for federal income tax credits. Beginning in 2022, homeowners got a
tax credit that covered up to 10% of the cost for insulation needs
and other energy needs, they also got a tax credit that is $300,
if they bought heating and cooling supplies.
